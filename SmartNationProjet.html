For this lesson we played around with the different PI modules such as the PIR Motion Sensor, Ultrasonic Sensor and Smoke sensor.
While trying out the different sensors, I personally found it frustrating when there were errors, as we were foreign to the codes and hardware. One of the obstacles I face is when we were connecting the MCP chip to the breadboard, we didn’t know we were supposed to connect the chip into the board hence we used the wires to connect it instead. Week 2 Lesson 2 For this lesson, we continued where we left off and connected the temperature and humidity sensor and the NFC Module. As we had made the mistake of not connecting the MCP Chip to the board, I was able to identify that the temperature and humidity sensor needed to be connect to the breadboard for it to work, due the missing component that was not provided. Therefore, connecting the hardware was smooth sailing. However, when we were suppose to run the program I faced many challenges such as some typo in the codes and not being patient enough to let the code run. As the hardware requires some time to get the data, my partner and I panicked for awhile as the program doesn’t show us any data, therefore we had to put print(“Hello world”) to see if our program is even running.
Overall I felt that I have learned a lot about being patient and understanding the how the codes communicates with the hardware.
